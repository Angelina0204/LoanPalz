1. System Overview & Problem Statement

LoanPalz implements a hybrid AI-based credit scoring framework that integrates machine learning predictions with behavioral financial analysis and rule-based decision logic. The system evaluates borrower creditworthiness by combining traditional financial indicators with newly designed behavioral credit indices to produce transparent, interpretable, and fair credit decisions.

Traditional credit scoring systems rely heavily on historical banking data. However, 1.4+ billion adults globally lack formal credit histories, leading to severe financial exclusion. Conventional models are rigid, black-box systems that often exhibit disparate impact across income groups and lack domain-driven financial reasoning.

Core Problem

Financial Exclusion & Rigidity

4. Behavioral Credit Indices (Core Innovation)

The proposed system introduces five behavioral indicators derived from financial theory and lending practices. These indices transform raw financial attributes into interpretable behavioral measures.

4.1 Financial Stability Index

Purpose: Measures long-term financial sustainability of the borrower.

MonthlyIncome / (DebtRatio × 1000 + NumberOfDependents + 1)

Interpretation: Higher value → stronger financial stability. Lower value → higher repayment risk. Estimates capacity to manage debt relative to income.

4.2 Payment Discipline Score

Purpose: Measures repayment reliability based on historical delays.

1 / (Late30 + Late60 + Late90 + 1)

Interpretation: Close to 1 → reliable borrower. Close to 0 → frequent delays. Captures behavioral repayment patterns.

4.3 Debt Stress Index

Purpose: Quantifies financial pressure from debt obligations.

DebtRatio × CreditUtilization × (OpenCreditLines + 1)

Interpretation: Higher value → greater financial stress. Lower value → manageable debt. Represents combined borrowing pressure.

4.4 Credit Utilization Risk

Purpose: Represents borrower dependence on credit resources.

CreditUtilizationRisk = RevolvingUtilization

Interpretation: Higher utilization indicates higher risk exposure.

4.5 Credit Experience Score

Purpose: Measures borrower credit experience.

CreditExperience = OpenCreditLines + RealEstateLoans

Interpretation: Higher experience indicates stronger credit history.

6. Hybrid Risk Decision System & Scoring

To increase interpretability and align predictions with financial domain knowledge, a rule-based adjustment layer is applied after model prediction to create a hybrid AI decision framework.

6.1 Adjustment Rules

The system modifies the predicted base risk using behavioral thresholds:

If PaymentDiscipline < 0.5: Risk += 0.10 If FinancialStability > threshold: Risk -= adjustment If DebtStress > threshold: Risk += adjustment If CreditExperience > threshold: Risk -= adjustment

These rules introduce domain knowledge and improve decision transparency.

6.2 Final Risk & Credit Score Generation

The final risk value is constrained between 0 and 1, then mapped to mimic industry credit scoring ranges.

Final Risk = Base Model Risk + Behavioral Adjustments
Credit Score = (1 − FinalRisk) × 600 + 300

Score Range: 300 – 900

6.3 Risk Classification

  • 0 – 0.3 → Low Risk
  • 0.3 – 0.6 → Medium Risk
  • > 0.6 → High Risk

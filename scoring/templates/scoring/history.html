System Transparency

Regulatory Compliance & Explainability

In automated financial decision-making, "black-box" models pose significant regulatory risks. This audit ledger exists to ensure complete algorithmic accountability. Every prediction is permanently logged alongside its underlying SHAP behavioral calculations and the specific hybrid-rules that triggered the final adjustment, allowing auditors and lenders to trace the exact mathematical lineage of any approved or denied credit line.

Closed-Loop Architecture

Continuous Learning Feedback

This history table serves as the foundational dataset for the system's closed-loop learning architecture. By storing the Base Risk (raw model output) adjacent to the Final Risk (domain-adjusted output), data scientists can continuously evaluate if the rule-engine is over-correcting the ML model. Over time, this logged telemetry is used to retrain the CatBoost model, further minimizing bias and improving baseline precision.
